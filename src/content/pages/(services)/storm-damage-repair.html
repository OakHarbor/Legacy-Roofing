Common Types of Storm Damage We Repair

Pacific Northwest weather patterns create various types of roof damage. Recognizing these issues and addressing them quickly prevents minor storm damage from escalating into major structural problems.

Wind Damage

Strong winds can lift shingles, damage flashing, tear off roof sections, and drive rain into vulnerable areas. Wind damage often appears subtle initially but compromises your roof's weather protection. We assess wind damage thoroughly, identifying both obvious and hidden issues that require repair.

Water Intrusion and Leaks

Heavy rain following storm damage can cause immediate water intrusion. Active leaks demand urgent attention to prevent interior damage, insulation saturation, and mold growth. Our emergency response includes temporary water mitigation followed by permanent repairs that restore complete weather protection.

Hail Damage

Hail impacts can bruise or crack shingles, damage flashing, and compromise your roof's protective granule layer. While hail damage may not cause immediate leaks, it reduces your roof's remaining lifespan and weather resistance. We provide thorough hail damage assessments and work with insurance adjusters to document all damage.

Fallen Tree and Debris Damage

Trees, branches, and storm debris can puncture roofing materials, damage structural components, and create immediate openings for water intrusion. We provide emergency tarping services to protect your home while coordinating debris removal and executing necessary repairs.
